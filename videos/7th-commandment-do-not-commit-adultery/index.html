7th Commandment: Do Not Commit Adultery

The seventh commandment protects the covenant of marriage — one of the most fundamental human relationships. But this isn't just about regulating sexual behavior. In the biblical story, marriage is a picture of God's faithful covenant love. Adultery is a betrayal of that covenant. In this video, we explore why sexual faithfulness matters, how it connects to Israel's relationship with God, and how Jesus takes the commandment to the level of the heart.

Reflect

Discussion Questions

  1. 1.The prophets often used adultery as a metaphor for Israel's spiritual unfaithfulness to God. How does understanding marriage as a picture of covenant help you understand why this commandment matters?
  2. 2.Jesus says that looking at someone with lust is equivalent to adultery in the heart (Matthew 5:27–28). How does that internal standard change how you think about faithfulness?
  3. 3.What habits, practices, or cultural norms make sexual faithfulness harder today? What would it look like to intentionally cultivate fidelity?
  4. 4.How does this commandment speak to those who have experienced the pain of infidelity — either as the one betrayed or the one who betrayed? What does the gospel say to both?
